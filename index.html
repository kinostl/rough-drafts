Online Collaborative Storytelling

What is Collaborative Storytelling?

Collaborative Storytelling is the act of creating stories with a group of likeminded individuals. You work together coming up with characters, locations, and history then combine them all together into finalized stories.

How is this different from Roleplaying?

You don't own the assets you create, your writing group does. In the same way, the writing group owns the story it creates. Everyone is an author to the story, and you don't always have the final say on a character you brought to the table.

What does Rough Drafts provide?

Writer's Groups

You can create a circle of friends who can collaborate on stories. These circles are the only ones who can see anything owned by the circle, until its ready for public view.

"Story Bible" Knowledge Bases

Rough Drafts provides you the tools to create a "Story Bible". A knowledge base that tracks the various characters in a story, the key locations within it, and major historical events that directly influence the ongoing story. Like any wiki, these knowledge bases provide a per-page history that can be rewound.

Discussions

A chatroom only accessible by the writers group. These are highly integrated to the Knowledge base, and provide tools to quickly preview elements being mentioned without needing to open a new page.

Drafts

Using a popcorn technique, members of the writers group take turns writing sections of the story and passing control to the next member.

Diaries

Individual members have a feed of notes that are private or public as they wish, useful for writing down thoughts, or sharing them with others. This is federated, and compatible with the federated network such as Mastodon.

Stories

Stories can be published right here on Rough Drafts, in a finalized form viewable by anyone you desire. Organized, comfortable to read, and easy to discover.
